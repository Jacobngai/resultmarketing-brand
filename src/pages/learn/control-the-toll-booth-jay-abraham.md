## The $400 Million Question

Jay Abraham charges $25,000 per person for a 90-minute consultation. In one session, he explained why most entrepreneurs work incredibly hard but never build real wealth.

The problem? They're selling products anyone else can sell.

> "If you're going to go to all of the trouble and all of the effort of setting up partnerships... it's probably better for you to search all of either Tokyo or the world and identify products that you can control yourself that are not being sold by everybody else."

This is the toll booth principle. And it separates business owners who build assets from those who just have jobs.

## The Commodity Trap

![The Commodity Trap - Competing With Everyone](/images/commodity-trap.png)

Jay was consulting with a Japanese woman who sold cosmetics through a network marketing company. She worked hard. She had ambition. But she had a fundamental problem:

> "You're taking a product that everybody else can sell and you don't have good profit margins so you have to work really hard to make any money."

She was competing with 10,000 other distributors in Japan selling the exact same products. Every time she convinced a medical clinic to sell her products, she was actually helping ALL distributors â€” because customers could buy from anyone.

### Why This Trap Is So Dangerous

1. **Low margins** â€” Multiple distribution layers mean you're fighting for scraps (10-30%)
2. **No competitive advantage** â€” Your marketing benefits competitors equally
3. **No sellable asset** â€” You're building THEIR business, not yours

> "You don't want to be one of 20,000 distributors in Japan selling that because every time you get a medical clinic to sell for you what they're really doing is selling for everybody because they're advertising your product but everybody else can sell it."

## The Toll Booth Position

![Distribution Ownership](/images/distribution-ownership.png)

Jay's alternative was simple but profound: **Control the product.**

> "If you can find great products â€” great beauty products that either exist in Japan or exist anywhere else in the world that can be easily and legally sold in Japan â€” then you could get the exclusive rights to them."

This is the toll booth. When you have exclusive rights, every sale must go through you. Every partner relationship you build benefits only YOU.

Jay reminded her of how Panasonic â€” now a $70+ billion company â€” started:

> "Remember I was talking about Panasonic yesterday... when they started they didn't have any products. Panasonic didn't invent anything. They controlled distribution. They owned the toll booth.

And here's what happens when you build distribution you own:

## How to Find Your Toll Booth

Jay's framework for escaping the commodity trap:

### Step 1: Search for Great Products with Bad Marketing

> "What I always do is I try to find great products with bad marketing and I try to tie up the rights to those products for my company."

Look for:
- Quality products with zero marketing presence in your market
- Companies focused on R&D but weak on distribution
- Foreign products not yet available in your region

### Step 2: Secure Exclusive Rights

The reason companies will give you exclusive rights? You bring distribution they don't have.

> "The reason that people would give you the exclusive rights are because you would control a distribution channel."

You're not asking for charity. You're offering access to 100+ partners they can't reach.

### Step 3: Build Distribution THEN Scale Products

Once you have distribution â€” medical clinics, salons, retailers, associations â€” you can add more products:

> "Now you have distribution that you can also gain control of a lot of other products that you can put through that distribution."

One distribution channel. Many products. That's leverage.
